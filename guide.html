Debt Repayment Guide

Snowball Method

The Snowball Method involves paying off debts from smallest to largest, regardless of interest rates. It provides psychological motivation by giving quick wins and building momentum.

Avalanche Method

The Avalanche Method prioritizes debts with the highest interest rates first. This approach minimizes overall interest payments, saving more money in the long run.

Debt Consolidation

Debt Consolidation combines multiple debts into one loan with a lower interest rate. It simplifies payments and may reduce the total amount of interest paid over time.

Balance Transfer

Balance Transfer involves moving high-interest debt to a credit card with a lower interest rate, often with an introductory 0% APR period. It can save money on interest charges if managed wisely.

Increase Income

Increasing income through additional work or side hustles provides more funds to allocate towards debt repayment. It accelerates the debt payoff process and reduces financial stress.

Budgeting

Creating a budget helps track expenses and identify areas to cut back, freeing up more money for debt payments. It's a crucial step in managing finances effectively.

Emergency Fund

Building an emergency fund safeguards against unexpected expenses and prevents reliance on credit cards during emergencies. It reduces the risk of falling back into debt.

Debt Snowflake

Debt Snowflake involves making small, extra payments towards debt whenever possible. This includes using spare change or cutting back on non-essential expenses to accelerate debt payoff.

Debt Counseling

Seeking guidance from a professional debt counselor offers personalized strategies and support for managing debt effectively. It provides valuable insights and resources for financial stability.

Debt Settlement

Debt Settlement involves negotiating with creditors to settle debts for less than the full amount owed. It can help alleviate financial burdens and expedite the path to debt freedom.
